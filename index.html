How we choose our picks

We do not run our own lab tests. Instead, we comb through thousands of long-term owner reviews on Amazon, Reddit, and specialty forums to learn what actually breaks, drifts, or disappoints after six months of use. We also compile expert opinions from publications like Wirecutter, CNET, and consumer-affairs sites that do perform controlled sensor comparisons. The monitors that make this list are the ones that consistently get praise for real-world accuracy, build quality, and dependable software, not just flashy features. We also track replacement-parts availability and customer-support responsiveness because a monitor you cannot fix or get help for is essentially e-waste. Products that require proprietary, hard-to-find sensors or that lose connectivity after a firmware update get bumped down. The three picks here all have a track record of manufacturer support and a community of happy owners who still recommend them years later.
